The Problem We're Solving

The infrastructure
hasn't kept pace.

Demand for wellness is strong and growing. But the infrastructure connecting people to it — and supporting the practitioners who deliver it — is fragmented, expensive, and exhausting to navigate.

Most practitioners are burning out on the business side of being in business before they've even begun working with clients. Managing multiple platforms, multiple costs, multiple logins, with no coherent presence and no real community behind them.

  • Fragmented tools that don't talk to each other
  • Marketing platforms that weren't built for wellness
  • No unified space to host courses, events, and community
  • No support system for the practitioner themselves
  • Financial instability that makes giving back feel impossible

Mindful Earth is being built to change that — one practitioner at a time.

About Mindful Earth

A wellness ecosystem
in development.

Mindful Earth is being built to give wellness practitioners — and everyone who supports the wellness experience — a single, coherent home. One profile, one platform, one place to build courses, host events, sell offerings, grow community, and be discovered.

The fragmentation ends here. The foundation we build for practitioners is the same foundation that allows them to give back. When practitioners thrive, the ripple goes outward — to their clients, their communities, and eventually the planet.

Right now we are in the early stages — offering services, building community, and developing the platform that will hold it all together. If you are a practitioner, a wellness venue, a mindful chef, a creative, or anyone who contributes to the wellness ecosystem — we would love to connect.

Meet the Founder

Montana King

Founder · Somatic Practitioner · Yoga Teacher · Wellness Consultant

I built Mindful Earth because I needed it to exist — and because I have lived on both sides of the problem it is trying to solve. I know what it costs to access healing when you don't have the money. And I know what it costs to be the practitioner, pouring yourself into others while trying to build something sustainable.

I'm a wellness professional with a background spanning life sciences, somatic practice, and business development. Over the past several years I've hosted retreats internationally, worked with practitioners and brands on growth and experience design, and developed a growing conviction that the wellness space needs better infrastructure — not just better content.

Mindful Earth is my response to that.

The Ginkgo
Our Symbol

Ancient. Resilient. Returning.

The Ginkgo is one of the oldest living tree species on earth — unchanged for over 200 million years, and one of the first trees to regrow after Hiroshima. I chose it because it felt like an honest reflection of what I'm building. Something slow, intentional, and deeply rooted. Something that survives because of its foundations, not despite them.

Mindful Earth is being built the same way. Slowly. With structure and clarity. And with the firm belief that what is rooted deeply enough will always return.

The Ripple

When practitioners
thrive, everyone benefits.

The Mindful Earth Community Access Fund is not the starting point — it is the result. When practitioners have the infrastructure to feel financially stable and professionally supported, they naturally have more to give.

The MECA exists for that moment. For practitioners who want to offer subsidised sessions, and for individuals for whom cost is a genuine barrier. Not charity bolted on — the organic outcome of a thriving ecosystem.

We are building the fund as the platform grows. If you would like to be part of that — as a contributor or a future recipient — register your interest below.
