Founder & Practitioner

Montana. King.

Yoga · Somatic Work · Energy Healing · Photography · Mentorship

I built Mindful Earth because I needed it to exist. And because I have lived — deeply — on both sides of the problem it is trying to solve.

I know what it costs to access healing when you don't have the money. I built my own practice through energy exchanges, community generosity, and the grace of people who believed wellness should be available to everyone. Without that, I would not be who I am today.

And I know what it costs to be the practitioner — pouring yourself into others while trying to build something sustainable, something that actually pays, something you can be proud of and live from. That exhaustion is real too.

Mindful Earth was built to hold both truths at once.

"Wellness practitioners deserve to be paid what they are worth. And wellness should be within reach for everyone. These two things are not in conflict — they are the mission."

The Vision Behind the Work

Mindful Earth is everything
I wished had existed.

When I was finding my way — through anxiety, through disconnection, through the long work of coming back to myself — there was no single place that held all of it. No ecosystem that supported the seeker and the practitioner at the same time.

So I built one. From a dream, from bracelets, from retreats in Canada, from years of learning what people actually need when they are trying to heal. From running towards the work even when the ground was shifting beneath me.

Mindful Earth is a network that holds the practitioner, the seeker, and the space between them. A place where someone building a practice can find the support, visibility, and community they need to thrive.

And where someone who has never been able to afford a retreat or a session has a pathway in — through the Community Access Fund, through community, through education that is genuinely free.

From running retreats in Canada to building this ecosystem in London, I have seen what happens when people are given the space to return to themselves. They become more. They give more. They care more — about each other and about the earth beneath their feet.

What to Expect

Not a session.
A shift.

Every person who works with me is unique — their path, their pace, their readiness. What I can promise is that I will meet you exactly where you are, without judgement, without agenda, and with everything I have. I work with a small number of people at a time, by design. This work deserves presence — not a production line.

01

To feel genuinely seen — not as a client, but as a whole person with a specific story and a specific path forward.

02
